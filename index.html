The Weekend

Two days, one jungle, and zero chance you'll want to leave.

Day One · Afternoon

Haldi & the Great Corbett Olympics

17 January · 2:00 PM onwards
Sunshine, turmeric, and full-blown chaos by the river.
Dress
Every shade of yellow you own — marigold, mustard, sunflower, lemon. The louder, the better.
Entry
No shades, no entry 😎 — it's about to get bright, yellow, and gloriously messy.
Games
Ladke Wale vs Ladki Wale. Tug of war, push-up face-offs, matka phod, lemon-'n'-spoon, sack race.
Warning: you'll arrive a guest and leave as turmeric.
Day One · Evening into Night

Mehendi Sundowner & Sangeet Night

17 January · 5:00 PM onwards
Golden hour by the water, dissolving into a starlit dance floor.
5:00–6:45
Mehendi, chai, mocktails, snacks & golden-hour photos.
7:00–10:00
Sangeet — performances, dancing & dinner under the stars.
Dress
Mehendi — greens & peach (sage, olive, bottle-green, coral). Sangeet — your call, wear your boldest.
Entry
Mehendi: nails done, phone charged — your hands are about to go out of service. Sangeet: bring your dance game.
The henna needs 90 minutes to set — that's your window to eat, flirt, and warm up your hips.
Day Two · The main event

The Wedding

18 January · games from the afternoon, pheras in the evening
The long way round finally arrives — vows by the river, under the sal trees.
Afternoon
Couple games — "Who's more likely to…", the Newlywed Game, joota chhupai (steal the groom's shoes), ring-in-the-milk.
Evening
The ceremony & dinner at Mango Bloom Riverview Resort.
Dress
Festive jewel tones — bring your finest.
Come early — the games start before the vows. Steal the shoes at your own risk.
